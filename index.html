The Scaling Gap

As companies grow, HR teams do not scale proportionally. The result: policy questions go unanswered, compliance gaps widen, and employee experience degrades — at precisely the moment culture matters most.

The Visibility Gap

HR leaders lack real-time insight into what employees are confused about, which policies are creating friction, and where compliance risk is building. Decisions are made on anecdote, not evidence.

Project Definition

Goal · Users · Scope

Project Goal

Demonstrate that an AI assistant grounded in company policy can resolve 80%+ of employee HR questions instantly — a threshold derived from SHRM research showing ~80% of HR queries are repetitive and policy-answerable. Surface compliance risk proactively. Deliver real-time HR operations intelligence — without adding headcount.

Primary Users

Employees — need fast, accurate policy answers without emailing HR.

HR Operations Managers — need actionable insight, not more dashboards to ignore.

How It Was Built

Single HTML file. Claude AI API (Anthropic). Zero backend infrastructure. Zero cost to deploy. Demonstrates that transformative tooling doesn't require a 6-month engineering sprint — it requires the right operational instincts and AI fluency.

What Makes Poli Different

Built on RevOps Principles

The same discipline that aligns Sales, Finance, and Product — applied to People Operations. Poli treats HR as a revenue-adjacent function: reduce friction, increase velocity, surface signal, automate the repeatable. This is what cross-functional operations fluency looks like applied to a new domain.

UX as a Design Constraint, Not an Afterthought

Every interaction was designed around cognitive load: option selectors instead of typing, tables instead of paragraphs, progressive disclosure in tooltips, one question at a time. The UX certification wasn't decorative — it's load-bearing infrastructure for how this tool works.

5-Phase AI Transformation Roadmap

Strategic Vision

Poli is not a chatbot. It is a proof of operational AI maturity — and a blueprint for enterprise-wide transformation. The strategy: start with the highest-friction, highest-volume, lowest-ambiguity process in a single business unit, prove the model, then replicate it systematically across every function. HR policy is the ideal first vertical — finite knowledge domain, measurable outcomes, universal employee touchpoint. Once this playbook is proven, it scales: Sales Ops, Finance, Legal, Customer Success — each vertical contributing to a unified, real-time executive view of organizational health, and a targeted mechanism for driving change where it matters most.
